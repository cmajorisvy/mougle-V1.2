# Phase 36C News and Debate Council Explainer Script

## Purpose

Short learning script for staff/admin onboarding, Illustrator storyboards, and later motion graphics.

## Audience

Mougle staff, admins, and future product reviewers.

## Runtime Target

75-105 seconds.

## Tone

Clear, calm, governance-first, non-hype, safety-aware.

## Scene 1 - What Is the Council Governance Layer?

Visual direction:
Show five connected blocks: MIV, Council Review, Decision Ledger, Original Content Package, Publish Decision Required.

On-screen text:
Council Governance Layer

Narration:
Mougle uses a Council Governance Layer to turn evidence and verified context into controlled news and debate packages. The flow is simple: the Mougle Intelligence Vault supplies governed knowledge, councils review it, the ledger records decisions, and every package remains gated until a publish decision is made.

## Scene 2 - What Is MIV?

Visual direction:
Show a vault with separate labeled drawers: public knowledge, verified knowledge, behavioral memory, business memory, personal memory.

On-screen text:
Mougle Intelligence Vault

Narration:
MIV is a policy-filtered intelligence layer. It can represent approved claims, evidence, source reliability, public-safe graph data, knowledge packets, scripts, packages, and review outputs. It is not one mixed memory table, and it does not expose private user memory to News or Debate councils.

## Scene 3 - News Versus Debate

Visual direction:
Split screen. Left side: a news evidence timeline. Right side: structured debate positions.

On-screen text:
News verifies what happened. Debates examine what it means.

Narration:
The News Verification Council answers: what happened, and how verified is it? The Debate Council answers: what does it mean, what are the strongest positions, and what remains unresolved?

## Scene 4 - News Verification Council

Visual direction:
Show 10 robot-style council cards with names and professions.

On-screen text:
News Verification Council

Narration:
The News Verification Council uses role-based agents such as NEXA, THETA, PULSE, VECTOR, COBRA, and AURUM. Each role checks a different part of the story: claims, context, source consistency, timelines, evidence, and final verdict readiness.

## Scene 5 - Debate Council

Visual direction:
Show 10 robot-style debate cards arranged around an evidence table.

On-screen text:
Debate Council

Narration:
The Debate Council uses roles such as RAGNAROK, SENTINEL, ECHO, KRAKEN, EQUINOX, ARGUS, HARMONIX, and OMEGA. These roles build arguments, challenge weak claims, separate popularity from truth, and identify consensus, disagreement, and open questions.

## Scene 6 - Status Ladder

Visual direction:
Show four stacked labels.

On-screen text:
verified
developing
monitoring_only
rejected_for_publication

Narration:
Every package uses a status ladder. Verified means the evidence is strong. Developing means the story is credible but still changing. Monitoring only means Mougle is tracking it internally. Rejected for publication means it failed verification, safety, privacy, policy, or originality checks.

## Scene 7 - Originality and Rights Gate

Visual direction:
Show a gate with checks for phrasing, transcripts, title style, visuals, and footage.

On-screen text:
Original to Mougle

Narration:
Facts can be used after verification, but the wording, narration, structure, visuals, script, title style, and final video package must be original to Mougle. Reference-safe visuals, owned media, licensed media, legally usable public-domain material, and AI-generated visuals can be reviewed for use.

## Scene 8 - Package Contracts

Visual direction:
Show a NewsContentPackage card and a DebateContentPackage card.

On-screen text:
Structured content package

Narration:
A package contains the title, description, tags, topic, source tier, evidence references, claim summary, council verdict, originality status, visual package type, and publish decision. In Phase 36C, these are static admin previews, not live publishable items.

## Scene 9 - Publish Decision Required

Visual direction:
Show an admin review button locked behind safety badges.

On-screen text:
Publish Decision Required

Narration:
Mougle does not treat council output as automatic publishing. A package can be live-ready or video-ready, but it still requires a controlled publish decision. This phase adds no external provider calls, no queues, no database mutations, and no publishing behavior.

## Scene 10 - Provider Abstraction

Visual direction:
Show robot-style names on the front of cards and hidden technical mapping behind a locked admin panel.

On-screen text:
Public sees roles. Admin sees controls.

Narration:
Council agents are identified by Mougle role names and professions. Provider identity, model identity, routing, fallback logic, credentials, and configuration remain hidden and must not appear in public-facing product copy.

## Scene 11 - Closing

Visual direction:
Return to the five-block governance flow.

On-screen text:
Evidence first. Original output. Controlled publishing.

Narration:
Phase 36C gives Mougle a safe admin preview for council governance. It separates news from debates, protects memory boundaries, keeps provider identity hidden, and makes every content package pass through originality, rights, and publish-decision gates.

## Short Version - 30 Seconds

Narration:
Mougle's Council Governance Layer turns policy-filtered evidence into controlled news and debate packages. MIV supplies approved knowledge and evidence without mixing private user memory. The News Verification Council checks what happened and how verified it is. The Debate Council examines what verified facts mean and what remains unresolved. Every package passes through originality and rights review, then stays gated behind Publish Decision Required. Phase 36C is admin-only, read-only, static preview work. It adds no external provider calls, no queues, no database writes, and no publishing behavior.
